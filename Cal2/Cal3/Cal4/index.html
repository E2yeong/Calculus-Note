5.1 The Area and Distance Problems

Core question: How can we find the area under a curve or total distance from velocity?

Integral = mathematical way of accumulating infinitely many small pieces.

Area under a curve

Divide [a,b] into n intervals (Δx = (b−a)/n). Approximate with rectangles:

\[ A ≈ \sum f(x_i^*) Δx, \quad \text{and } \lim_{n→∞} \sum f(x_i^*)Δx = \int_a^b f(x)\,dx \]

The definite integral is defined as the limit of Riemann sums.

Example

\( f(x)=x^2, [0,2] \Rightarrow \int_0^2 x^2 dx = [x^3/3]_0^2 = 8/3 \)

Connection with Motion

Total displacement: \(\int_a^b v(t)dt\)
Total distance: \(\int_a^b |v(t)|dt\)

Integration accumulates change: area → distance → total quantity.

5.2 The Definite Integral

Formal definition:

\[ \int_a^b f(x)dx = \lim_{n→∞}\sum_{i=1}^{n} f(x_i^*)Δx \]

Properties

  • ∫ₐᵃ f(x) dx = 0
  • ∫ₐᵇ f(x) dx = −∫ᵇₐ f(x) dx
  • ∫ₐᵇ (f+g)dx = ∫ₐᵇ f + ∫ₐᵇ g
  • ∫ₐᵇ c·f = c∫ₐᵇ f
Integral gives signed area: positive above x-axis, negative below.

Example

\( \int_0^2 (2x−1)dx = [x^2−x]_0^2 = 2 \)

5.3 Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (FTC)

The bridge between differentiation and integration.

Part 1 — Differentiation of an Integral

If \( F(x) = \int_a^x f(t)dt \), then \( F'(x) = f(x) \)

Part 2 — Evaluating a Definite Integral

\( \int_a^b f(x)dx = F(b) − F(a), \) where \( F'(x)=f(x) \)

This allows us to compute integrals without limits of sums—using antiderivatives instead.

Example

\( \int_1^3 2x dx = [x^2]_1^3 = 8 \)

5.4 Indefinite Integrals & Net Change Theorem

Definition

\( \int f(x)dx = F(x) + C \)

C = constant of integration (disappears under differentiation).

Net Change Theorem

\( \int_a^b F'(x)dx = F(b) − F(a) \)

Integrating a rate of change gives total change (velocity→displacement, acceleration→velocity).

Example

v(t)=3t², [0,2]: \( \int_0^2 3t²dt = [t³]_0^2 = 8 \)

5.5 The Substitution Rule

The reverse of the Chain Rule. Let \(u=g(x), du=g'(x)dx\):

\( \int f(g(x))g'(x)dx = \int f(u)du \)

Examples

1️⃣ \( \int 2x\cos(x²)dx → u=x², du=2x dx → \int \cos(u)du = \sin(u)+C = \sin(x²)+C \)

2️⃣ \( \int_0^1 2x e^{x²}dx → u=x², du=2x dx → [e^u]_0^1 = e−1 \)

Use when an inner function and its derivative appear together.

6.1 Areas Between Curves

Area = \(\int_a^b [Top(x) − Bottom(x)] dx\)

Example

f(x)=x², g(x)=x, [0,1]: \( \int_0^1 (x−x²)dx = [x²/2−x³/3]_0^1 = 1/6 \)

6.2 Volumes by Revolution — Disk & Washer Methods

Disk Method

\( V = π\int_a^b [R(x)]² dx \)

Washer Method

\( V = π\int_a^b ([R_{outer}]² − [R_{inner}]²)dx \)

Example

y=√x, about x-axis [0,4]: \(V = π∫_0^4 x dx = [πx²/2]_0^4 = 8π\)

6.3 Cylindrical Shells

\( V = 2π\int_a^b (radius)(height)dx \)

Example

y=x, y=0, x=1 rotated about y-axis: \(V = 2π∫_0^1 x·x dx = 2π/3\)

Use shells for vertical (y-axis) rotations — simpler than disk/washer here.
