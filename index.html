1. Limits at Infinity & Asymptotes

Horizontal Asymptote: If \( \lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = L \), then the graph approaches \(y=L\).

Vertical Asymptote: If \( \lim_{x \to a^\pm} f(x) = \pm \infty \), then the line \(x=a\) is a vertical asymptote.

Continuity Conditions:

  1. \(f(a)\) is defined
  2. \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x)\) exists
  3. \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a)\)

Types of Discontinuities: removable, jump, infinite.

2. Derivative: Definition

\( f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} \)

Example: \( f(x)=x^2 \) â†’ \( f'(x)=2x \).

3. Differentiability

Differentiability requires continuity. But continuity does not always imply differentiability.

  • Sharp corner: \(y=|x|\) at \(x=0\)
  • Cusp: slope â†’ \(\pm\infty\)
  • Vertical tangent

4. Basic Derivative Rules

  • Power Rule: \((x^n)' = nx^{n-1}\)
  • Product Rule: \((fg)' = f'g + fg'\)
  • Quotient Rule: \(\left(\frac{f}{g}\right)'=\frac{f'g-fg'}{g^2}\)
  • Sum/Difference Rule: derivative distributes

5. Chain Rule

\(\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du}\cdot \frac{du}{dx}\)

Example: \( y=(3x^2+1)^5 \) â†’ \( y'=30x(3x^2+1)^4 \).

6. Derivatives of Common Functions

  • \((\sin x)'=\cos x\)
  • \((\cos x)'=-\sin x\)
  • \((e^x)'=e^x\)
  • \((\ln x)'=\frac{1}{x},\ x>0\)

7. Higher-Order Derivatives

Second derivative: \(f''(x)\). Indicates concavity, acceleration in physics.

General: \( f^{(n)}(x), \ \frac{d^n y}{dx^n} \).

8. Implicit Differentiation

Example: \(x^2+y^2=25\) â†’ \(2x+2y\frac{dy}{dx}=0 \Rightarrow \frac{dy}{dx}=-\frac{x}{y}\).

10. Applications of Derivatives

  • Tangent line: \(y-f(a)=f'(a)(x-a)\)
  • Rate of change: average = \(\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}\), instantaneous = \(f'(a)\)
  • Physics: \(s(t)\) â†’ \(v=s'(t)\) â†’ \(a=s''(t)\)
  • Optimization: maximize/minimize cost, revenue, area
  • Concavity: \(f''(x)>0\) convex up, \(f''(x)<0\) convex down, inflection point when sign changes
